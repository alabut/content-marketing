Engineering Growth

Handling millions of API calls every day is not a simple feat. By the end of 2013, Zapier was run by over 70 servers on Amazon's Web Service, up from a mere dozen or so at the beginning of 2013.

Zapier is all about chosing the right tool for the specific job at hand. On the engineering side, that goes double. We employ several different databases (MySQL, Redis, Elasticsearch, RethinkDB, RedShift), a handful of different frameworks (Django, SQLAlchemy, Puppet, Backbone.js) and of course a few of our favorite languages (Python, Coffeescript/Javascript, Ruby). If it's the right tool for the job, we'll use it.

All of this is accomplished with only 4 engineers on the team!

February Redesign

The January version of Zapier was drag-and-drop and playful. The homepage featured the Zap editor and the hypothesis was that being able to get started using the product right away would be best for user conversions. (alt: The homepage allowed users to immediately begin making integrations; our hypothesis was that if the faster they began using the product, the more likely they’d be to sign up.)

But, unfortunately, that wasn't the case. We learned:

  • Drag-and-drop functionality is hard to build and use on mobile devices
  • People have been conditioned to look for an obvious sign up button on landing pages, and when they can't easily find one, they leave the site quickly

Focused on remedying these two issues, we implemented a more traditional homepage with an obvious call-to-action to sign up and scrapped our drag-and-drop editor so that the user could do everything with simple click or touch actions.

These simple changes brought conversion rates for new signups to active user from 34% to 50%. That's a huge bump!

November Redesign

Though the February redesign was an improvement, we felt Zapier was still limping along as an engineering-driven team without ample design resources. It was obvious that if we could bring on a full-time designer, we'd be able to reap the rewards of having someone who is an expert in their field, making Zapier easier to use and easier to remember.

Thus, just a few weeks ago we relaunched Zapier as the web app that gives you internet superpowers.

The new design boosted visit-to-signup conversion rates by over 25% and we're seeing increased engagement with the app as well. Remember, don't undervalue design.

SUPPORTING 150,000 USERS

Excellent support is a crucial value add we strive to provide at Zapier. We believe:

  1. No one really wants to require support
  2. Sometimes you need support, and when you do, that experience should be great

Zapier has always been support-driven development and in March we formalized the process to help speed up ticket responses to help address belief No. 2.But, this improved system to offer support didn't address belief No. 1.

So, in July, to empower people to find answers to questions themselves, we buckled down and wrote help documentation for as many integrations as possible. Instantly, the number of support queries we received decreased. Of course, our user base kept growing, so while the percentage of users who send in requests is lower than before, the total quantity is going up again. (It’s a good problem to have.)
