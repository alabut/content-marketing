FROM 120 TO 250

At the end of December 2012 there was roughly 120 supported services on Zapier, most of which had been added by us. But realizing it was best for users if others could add integrations in August of 2012 we launched the Zapier developer platform.

Building for & supporting a growing user base

Generating 150,000 users doesn't come without its challenges. Here's some stats and milestones to demonstrate the challenges and success of going from 38,000 to 150,000 users in one year.

SECTION ON FEBRUARY REDESIGN

2013 got off to a bang with a fresh redesign. The January version of Zapier was drag-and-drop and playful. The home page featured the app and the hypothesis was that being able to get started right away would be best for conversions.

But unfortunately that wasn't the case. It turns out drag-and-drop is really hard on mobile devices (actually impossible). People have also been conditioned to look for a signup button and when one wasn't easily found they bounced quickly.

So the February redesign focused on fixing those two things. We implemented a more traditional homepage with an obvious call-to-action for signing up and we also nuked drag-and-drop everywhere so that click or touch actions could allow the user to do everything. Those simple changes brought conversion rates for new signups to active user from 34% to 50%. A huge bump.

SECTION ON NOVEMBER REDESIGN

While the February redesign was an improvement, Zapier was still limping along as an engineering driven team without a much design help. It was pretty obvious that if we could bring on a full-time designer we'd be able to reap the rewards of having someone who is an expert in their field making Zapier easier to use and easier to remember.

Thus just a few weeks ago we relaunched Zapier as the web app that gives you internet superpowers. The new design has already boosted visit to signup conversion rates by over 25% and we're seeing more engagement with the app. Remember kids, don't undervalue design.

SUPPORTING 150,000 USERS

Support is one of the huge value adds we strive to provide at Zapier. There's a couple things we truly believe.

  1. No one really wants to have to talk to support
  2. Sometimes you have to talk to support, and hopefully that experience is great

One thing we've always done at Zapier is support drive development, but in March we formalized the process to help speed up ticket responses to help address belief number 2. Our average response time compared to the previous month improved immediately from 5 hours, 37 minutes to 3 hours, 55 minutes. But having more a better system to do support didn't address belief number 1.

So in July of 2012 we buckled down and wrote help docs for as much of the 250 integrations we support as possible. And instantly the number of support queries we received starting going down. Fortunately we kept growing and while the percent of users who send in requests is lower than before the total quantity is going up again. In 2014 we'll be experimenting with a few new things to try and provide the best overall support experience possible.
